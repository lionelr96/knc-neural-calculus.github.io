Michael I. Ivanitsky

About me

I graduated from the University of Michigan in April 2020 with a math degree and a CS minor. I'm currently taking a gap year to finish and publish some work. I intend to pursue a graduate degree in computational neuroscience in the future. In general, I'm interested in how biological neural networks learn. These days I'm working on novel algorithms for training SNNs, with a soft spot for the HH model ❤. I've recently written a paper with James Hazeden (he should have a page on this site, James if you see this bug me to link you) and Daniel Forger on using SGD to train a layered network of HH neurons. That may or may not appear at a NeurIPS Workshop. I'll need to update on how that goes. After that I'm hoping to work on something related to training using eligibility propogation, neuromodulated STDP, or dendritic morphology. I've also got some work in game theory and combinatorics that has been "almost done" for months now.

I also have a bunch of random tools and stuff up on my github. I've previously done some work in simulating connectome generation, neuromodulation of networks, neuroevolution, and ML for biological image segmentation. If you're interested in hearing more about any of these, I'm more than happy to rant about them over email.
